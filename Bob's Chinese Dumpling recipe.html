Bob’s Chinese Pot Sticker Dumplings

Makes about 50


Wonton Wraps: This recipe should fill about one package of Melissa brand wonton wraps (because this is a “putsy” recipe, I usually double it and make a large batch of about 100 all at once).


Filling:

2 lbs ground turkey

1 can water chestnuts, finely chopped

2 bunches of green onions, finely chipped

1 ½ T of five spice powder (or one teaspoon each of anise seed, fennel seed, ground cloves, ground cinnamon and ground or grated ginger)

4 T soy sauce

1 T sugar

½ T sesame oil


  1. Mix all the filling ingredients together and spread out in a frying pan and brown both sides (the original recipe calls for putting the filling into the wonton wrap raw but I like to cook the filling because I don’t like to over steam the dumpling in step #4 – this also assures the filling is fully cooked).

  2. Spoon about a tablespoon of the cooked filling onto a wonton wrap. Fold over the top and press the edges together firmly (form a triangle). Roll over the corners and glue edges together with water (a moistened finger does the trick).

  3. Pour about a tablespoon of oil in a non-stick fry pan (350 degrees F) to place each dumpling in before sliding them into their browning position in the fry pan (this puts a small film of oil on the bottom of each dumpling to help them brown). Fill the fry pan with dumplings to brown (but avoid having them touch each other or they’ll stick together when steamed). Brown the bottom of the dumplings in the fry pan.


  1. When the underside of each dumpling is brown, add about 1/8 to 1/4 cup water to the pan and cover it tightly. Continue cooking (steaming) for about 1 or 2 minutes, or until the top surface of dumplings are steamed and appears cooked (translucent).


Dipping Sauce:

½ cup of soy sauce

½ peanut oil

1 bunch of green onions, finely chopped

1T of sugar


Serve immediately!


The cooked dumplings freeze well in a Tupperware container so this hors d’oeuvre can be made ahead of time and stored for an upcoming occasion, then thawed and served at room temperature.
